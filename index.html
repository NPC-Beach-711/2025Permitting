Modernizing U.S. Energy Infrastructure Permitting

Project Permitting Innovation: A Framework for Reforming Energy Infrastructure Approvals provides practical recommendations to modernize how energy infrastructure is reviewed, authorized, and built in the United States.

The study responds to a request from the U.S. Secretary of Energy to identify policy, regulatory, and process improvements that can accelerate deployment of energy infrastructure while maintaining strong environmental stewardship and community engagement.

At a Glance

  • Streamlines overlapping federal, state, and local reviews.
  • Improves transparency and predictability for project sponsors.
  • Supports timely deployment of critical energy infrastructure.
  • Retains strong environmental protections and community input.

Key Themes

A System Under Strain

Existing permitting processes struggle with the scale and complexity of today’s energy transition, contributing to delays and uncertainty for needed infrastructure.

Clarity and Coordination

Clear roles, coordinated schedules, and early issue resolution can accelerate reviews without weakening environmental standards.

Data, Tools, and Capacity

Modern digital tools, reliable data, and adequately resourced agencies are essential for consistent, timely decisions.

Community Engagement

Early, meaningful engagement with communities strengthens projects and builds trust while advancing national energy objectives.

About the National Petroleum Council

The National Petroleum Council (NPC) is a federal advisory committee established to advise the U.S. Secretary of Energy on matters related to oil, natural gas, and other energy issues.

NPC studies draw on expertise from industry, academia, non-governmental organizations, and other stakeholders. The Council’s role is advisory; it does not set policy or advocate for specific outcomes.

For more information, visit: www.npc.org.
